SURVIVAL TO 21s
Before civilization.
Before language.
Before the first human eye ever opened to the light —
there was survival.
Every creature that ever lived passed a single inheritance
to its descendants:

The unbreakable will to persist.

You carry four billion years of that inheritance.
Every ancestor you have — without exception —
survived long enough for you to exist.
The enemies change. The obstacles evolve.
What once hunted us through ancient forests
now arrives as pressure, chaos, uncertainty —
the relentless forces of a world that waits for no one.

But the primal response remains the same:
Move. Adapt. Keep going.
Camus wrote that we must imagine Sisyphus happy —
not because the boulder stops rolling,
but because the struggle itself is enough.

To survive is not merely to exist.
It is to insist on your presence
in the face of everything that would erase it.
21 seconds.
It sounds like nothing.

But every second you hold on
is a declaration:

I am still here.
